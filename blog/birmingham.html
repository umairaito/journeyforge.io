Birmingham, England's second city, is a vibrant destination packed with history, culture, and incredible food. From industrial heritage to modern marvels, here's our guide to the 10 best things to do in Birmingham.

1. Explore the Birmingham Canal Network

Birmingham has more canals than Venice! Take a boat tour or simply stroll along the historic Brindleyplace and Gas Street Basin. The canal walks are perfect for a peaceful afternoon with scenic waterside pubs and restaurants.

2. Visit Birmingham Museum & Art Gallery

One of the finest museums in the UK, featuring over 40 galleries with world-class collections. Don't miss the Pre-Raphaelite paintings and the famous "The Telegram" collection. Best of all â€” it's free!

3. Discover the Jewellery Quarter

Step back in time in this historic quarter where jewellery has been made since the 1800s. Visit the Museum of the Jewellery Quarter, watch live demonstrations, and perhaps pick up a unique piece. Over 600 jewellery businesses still operate here!

4. Explore Birmingham's Bullring & Selfridges

The Bullring is one of the UK's largest shopping centres with over 200 stores. The iconic Selfridges building, with its futuristic facade, is a must-see. It's a shopaholic's paradise in the heart of the city.

5. Tour the Birmingham Back to Backs

Experience life in Victorian England at this preserved courtyard of working-class houses. Run by the National Trust, these historic homes offer a fascinating glimpse into Birmingham's industrial past.

6. Enjoy Birmingham's Food Scene

Birmingham is famous for its diverse food scene, especially the Balti Triangle. Try authentic curries, Caribbean jerk chicken, or fresh Vietnamese pho. The city has something for every palate and budget.

7. Visit Cadbury World

Just a short bus ride from the city centre, Cadbury World is a chocolate lover's dream. Learn about the history of Cadbury, see chocolate being made, and enjoy interactive exhibits. Book tickets in advance!

8. Explore the Custard Factory

In the heart of Digbeth, this creative quarter is packed with independent shops, studios, and cafes. It's Birmingham's hub for artists, designers, and vintage lovers. Great for unique souvenirs and brunch.

9. Take a Walk Through Sutton Park

One of the largest urban parks in Europe, Sutton Park offers 2,400 acres of beautiful countryside. Perfect for walking, cycling, or a picnic. There are even ancient monuments and a medieval church within the grounds.

10. Catch a Show at the Hippodrome

Birmingham's premier theatre hosts West End musicals, ballet, and comedy. The Hippodrome is also home to the Birmingham Royal Ballet. Check what's on during your visit for a unforgettable night out.

Best Time to Visit Birmingham

Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November) offer mild weather and fewer crowds. Summer (June-August) has festivals and events but can be busy. Winter brings Christmas markets and ice skating.

Getting Around Birmingham

  • Train: New Street Station is central with excellent connections
  • Tram: West Midlands Metro serves city centre and suburbs
  • Bus: Extensive network, National Express West Midlands
  • Walk: City centre is very walkable
