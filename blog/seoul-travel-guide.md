# The Ultimate Seoul Travel Guide for 2026

Seoul is a city where ancient temples sit alongside futuristic skyscrapers, and street food vendors serve up Michelin-starred meals. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned Korea traveler, this guide will help you experience the best of South Korea's vibrant capital.

---

## Best Time to Visit Seoul

**Spring (March-May)** and **Autumn (September-November)** are the ideal times to visit Seoul. The weather is mild, and the city comes alive with cherry blossoms in spring and stunning fall foliage in autumn.

- **Spring:** Cherry blossoms peak in late March to early April
- **Summer:** Hot and humid (June-August) — indoor attractions recommended
- **Autumn:** Golden maples everywhere, comfortable temperatures
- **Winter:** Cold but magical, with Christmas markets and ice skating

---

## Top Things to Do in Seoul

### 1. Explore Historic Palaces
Start your journey at **Gyeongbokgung Palace**, the largest of Seoul's five grand palaces. Arrive early to watch the changing of the guard ceremony. Nearby **Bukchon Hanok Village** offers a glimpse into traditional Korean architecture.

### 2. Shop Till You Drop
- **Myeongdong** — Fashion and cosmetics paradise
- **Insadong** — Art, crafts, and antique shops
- **Hongdae** — Youth culture, indie music, and street art

### 3. Experience Korean Street Food
No trip to Seoul is complete without exploring its street food scene:
- **Tteokbokki** (spicy rice cakes)
- **Kimbap** (Korean sushi rolls)
- **Hotteok** (sweet filled pancakes)
- **Fried chicken** — Seoul is famous for it!

### 4. Get a Views
- **N Seoul Tower** — Panoramic city views
- **Lotte World Tower** — World's 5th tallest building
- **Hangang River** — Perfect for evening walks

### 5. Immerse in K-Culture
- Watch a **K-pop recording** live
- Rent a hanbok (traditional dress) in Bukchon
- Visit **KBS World** for TV show experiences

## Budget Tips

- **Seoul Metro:** Buy a T-Money card for easy transit
- **Meals:** Street food is delicious and cheap (~$5-10 per meal)
- **Accommodation:** Hostels start at $20/night
- **Free attractions:** Parks, markets, palace exterior views

## Getting Around

- **Subway:** Fast, cheap, and English-friendly
- **Taxis:** Affordable, use Kakao T app
- **AREX:** Express train from Incheon Airport (45 min to Seoul Station)
